Understanding Fractions Made Easy

⭐ Addition of Like Fractions ⭐

1. Introduction (Do you know that …)

...you already know how to add like fractions? If you can count, you can do this! "Like fractions" are fractions that are part of the same family because they share the same denominator. Adding them is as simple as counting pieces of the same size.

Example: What is ³/₈ + ²/₈?

Imagine Emeka eats ³/₈ of a pizza 🍕, and Funke eats ²/₈ of the same pizza. Since all the slices are the same size (eighths), we can just count the total number of slices eaten!

2. 5 places where the above topic is used in our daily life with example.

Sharing Food

If you eat ¹/₄ of a cake and your friend eats ¹/₄, you have both eaten ¹/₄ + ¹/₄ = ²/₄ (or half) of the cake.

Completing a Task

Amina does ³/₁₀ of her homework in the morning and ⁴/₁₀ in the afternoon. In total, she has completed ³/₁₀ + ⁴/₁₀ = ⁷/₁₀ of her homework.

Measuring and Sewing

A tailor uses ³/₈ of a yard of fabric for a pocket and ¹/₈ of a yard for a collar. He has used ³/₈ + ¹/₈ = ⁴/₈ (or half) a yard in total.

Using Fuel

If a car uses ¹/₄ of a tank of petrol to go to the market and ¹/₄ of a tank to come back, it has used ¹/₄ + ¹/₄ = ²/₄ of a tank.

Tracking Time

If a football match half is ³/₄ of an hour and added time is ¹/₄ of an hour, the total time played is ³/₄ + ¹/₄ = ⁴/₄, which is 1 whole hour.

3. Key terms and definitions.

Knowing these simple terms will make you a fractions expert in no time! 🎓

Like Fractions

These are fractions that have the exact same denominator. They are from the same "family". Examples: ¹/₈, ³/₈, and ⁷/₈ are all like fractions.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction. It tells you how many pieces you have. In ⁵/₆, the numerator is 5.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction. It tells you the size of the pieces (how many make a whole). In ⁵/₆, the denominator is 6.

4. Step-by-step explanations and calculations

Problem: Add ³/₈ + ²/₈

This is the simplest kind of fraction addition. Follow these easy steps!

Step 1 – Add the Numerators.

The top numbers are the only ones you need to add.
3 + 2 = 5.

Step 2 – Keep the Denominator.

The denominator shows the size of the slices, and that does not change. So, it stays as 8.

Step 3 – Write the Final Fraction.

Combine the new numerator and the original denominator.

The final answer is ⁵/₈.

5. Real-life situation

Problem: At a birthday party, Bola cuts a cake into 12 equal slices. Amina eats ²/₁₂ of the cake, and Emeka eats ³/₁₂. What fraction of the cake did they eat altogether?

Step 1: Add the numerators. We are adding the number of slices they ate.
2 (Amina's slices) + 3 (Emeka's slices) = 5.

Step 2: Keep the denominator. The cake was cut into 12 slices, so the denominator stays 12.

Final Answer: Together, they ate ⁵/₁₂ of the cake.

6. Financial literacy problem

Problem: Chinedu is saving his pocket money. In the first week, he saved ¹/₁₀ of his goal. In the second week, he saved ³/₁₀ of his goal. If his total savings goal is ₦5,000, how much money has he saved so far?

Part 1: Find the total fraction of his goal saved.

Add the fractions: ¹/₁₀ + ³/₁₀.
Add numerators: 1 + 3 = 4.
Keep the denominator: 10.
He has saved ⁴/₁₀ of his goal.

Part 2: Calculate the amount of money saved.

We need to find ⁴/₁₀ of ₦5,000.
Amount Saved = ⁴/₁₀ × 5,000.
This means (4 × 5,000) ÷ 10 = 20,000 ÷ 10 = 2,000.

Final Answer: Chinedu has saved ₦2,000 so far.

7. Mathematics tricks

Trick: Simplify Before You Finish!

Sometimes, your answer can be simplified to an easier fraction.

Example: Add ³/₈ + ¹/₈.

Step 1: Add the fractions normally.
3 + 1 = 4. The answer is ⁴/₈.

Step 2: Check if you can simplify.
Can both the numerator (4) and the denominator (8) be divided by the same number? Yes! They can both be divided by 4.
4 ÷ 4 = 1.
8 ÷ 4 = 2.

Final Simplified Answer: The simplest way to write the answer is ½. It's the same value, just neater!

8. Common mistakes to avoid

Mistake 1: Adding the Denominators

This is the #1 error! Remember, the denominator just tells you the name of the fraction family.

Problem: ²/₇ + ³/₇

Wrong Way ❌: (2+3) / (7+7) = ⁵/₁₄. This is incorrect!

Right Way ✅: Keep the denominator. (2+3) / 7 = ⁵/₇.

Mistake 2: Changing the Numerator Randomly

Some students feel they need to change everything.

Problem: ¹/₅ + ²/₅

Wrong Way ❌: Thinking you need to find an LCM and changing the fractions to ⁵/₂₅ + ¹⁰/₂₅. This is unnecessary work and can lead to errors.

Right Way ✅: If the denominators are already the same, just add the numerators. It's that simple! ³/₅.

9. Practice & Challenge Zone

Practice Question

What is ⁴/₉ + ²/₉?

Add the numerators: 4 + 2 = 6.

Keep the denominator: ⁶/₉.

Bonus Simplification: Both 6 and 9 can be divided by 3. 6÷3=2 and 9÷3=3. The simplest answer is ²/₃.

🤯 Challenge Problem

The school farm is divided into 15 plots. Class JSS 1A planted maize on ⁴/₁₅ of the plots. Class JSS 1B planted cassava on ³/₁₅ of the plots. Class JSS 1C planted yams on ²/₁₅ of the plots. What fraction of the farm is still unplanted?

Hint: First, add the fractions of the farm that have been planted. Then, remember that the whole farm is ¹⁵/₁₅. Subtract the planted fraction from the whole.

Step 1: Find the total fraction planted.
Add the fractions for all three classes: ⁴/₁₅ + ³/₁₅ + ²/₁₅.
Add numerators: 4 + 3 + 2 = 9.
The total planted fraction is ⁹/₁₅.

Step 2: Find the unplanted fraction.
The whole farm is ¹⁵/₁₅. Subtract the planted part from the whole: ¹⁵/₁₅ - ⁹/₁₅.
Subtract numerators: 15 - 9 = 6.

Final Answer (Unsimplified): The unplanted fraction is ⁶/₁₅.

Final Answer (Simplified): Both 6 and 15 can be divided by 3. 6÷3=2 and 15÷3=5. The simplest answer is ⅖ of the farm is unplanted.

10. Summary of steps

  • Step 1: ADD THE NUMERATORS - The top numbers.
  • Step 2: KEEP THE DENOMINATOR - The bottom number stays the same.
  • Step 3: SIMPLIFY - If possible, simplify the fraction to its lowest terms.
